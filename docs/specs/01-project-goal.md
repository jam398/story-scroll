# Spec 01: Project Goal

## Purpose

Define the user-facing goal, audience, and completion standard for the redesigned League of Legends presentation page.

## What Done Looks Like

- The site presents a short, factual history of League of Legends as a slide-driven scroll presentation.
- The experience feels image-led, paced, and cinematic rather than article-like.
- The page works on desktop and mobile.
- The result is original in design and does not clone Riot or the reference presentation repo too closely.
- The site builds as a static export for GitHub Pages.

## Constraints

- Scope is one static page, not a multi-product app.
- The story must stay compact, roughly 8 to 12 slides or beats, with slide merges allowed when stronger imagery and pacing make the story easier to follow.
- The implementation must be reviewable in small phases.
- The content must remain grounded in verified source material.
- The reading experience must stay scan-friendly; each slide should feel brief, visual, and easy to progress through instead of dense or essay-like.
- The page should prioritize images, spatial pacing, and short copy over long narrative blocks.
- The reader should feel the next scene arrive visually before the text becomes the main point of attention.

## Non-Goals

- No dashboard, auth, database, API, or CMS.
- No speculative lore or unsupported history additions.
- No direct cloning of reference repo visuals or motion signatures.
- No deck with 20-plus thin slides that dilute the story.

## Acceptance Checks

- A reader can understand the page purpose from the opening slide and closing slide.
- All planned beats are represented or intentionally merged with justification.
- The page preserves a clear narrative arc from origin to present significance.
- The content reads as a visual presentation, not as a stack of article cards.
- The sequence feels background-led first and text-led second, rather than showing every image and paragraph at full emphasis at the same moment.
- The final implementation remains within the single-page project scope.
